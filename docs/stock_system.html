System Architecture

The Intelligent Stock System

A comprehensive guide to how PAPs n POPs tracks, calculates, and presents inventory data to power your bar operations with absolute precision.

1. The Core Concept: Fluidity vs Solid

In traditional retail systems, everything is a "Piece". You buy 1 apple, you sell 1 apple. But in a liquor store and bar, you buy a 750ml bottle, and you might sell it as a full bottle, or pour it out as 25 individual shots.

PAPs n POPs handles this natively by splitting products into two fundamental categories:

Piece Products

Items sold exactly as they are received. e.g. A can of Coca-Cola, a pack of cigarettes. The database tracks the total Count.

Volume Products

Liquor that can be poured. The database tracks the total Milliliters (ml) in the building, completely ignoring the physical bottles until display time.

2. The Math: Decoding "2 Bottles, 17 Shots"

When the system calculates your inventory, it uses a very specific algorithm to make the data physically understandable to you as the owner.

Scenario: 2010 ml of Jameson in stock

  1. 1
    Calculate Full Containers
    The system knows a "Full Bottle" is 750ml. It divides the total volume by the container size.
    2010 / 750 = 2 full bottles (1500 ml)
  2. 2
    Isolate the Remainder
    It subtracts the full bottles from the total to find what is sitting in the open bottle.
    2010 - 1500 = 510 ml remaining
  3. 3
    Format the Remainder
    Instead of saying "510ml", it finds your smallest variant (a 30ml Shot) and divides the remainder.
    510 / 30 = 17 Shots
Final Display: 2 Bottles, 17 Shots left

3. The Variants List vs Total Stock

When you tap into a Product Detail screen, you see two very different sets of numbers. This is an intentional UI design to serve two different psychological needs.

The Hero Summary

"2 Bottles, 17 Shots left"

  • Provides a physical picture of the shelf.
  • Used for physical stock-taking and auditing.

The Variant Yields

"67 Shots", "33 Doubles"

  • Shows the maximum potential if the entire stock was converted to one variant.
  • Tells the cashier exactly how many of that specific item they can sell right now.

4. Day-to-Day Operational Impact

Fraud Prevention & Exact Auditing

Because the system tracks down to the milliliter, bartenders can no longer "hide" missing stock in rounding errors. If the system says there should be 17 shots in the open bottle, you can visually verify the bottle level (about 2/3rds full) and immediately spot discrepancies.

Frictionless POS Experience

The cashier never has to do math. They just tap "Double" or "Full Bottle". The database trigger automatically deducts the correct milliliter amount. This prevents cashier errors during busy nights.

Receiving Made Easy

When receiving stock, you don't receive "15,000 ml". The Receiving screen asks how many "Bottles" you received, and automatically multiplies it by the `container_size` behind the scenes. It speaks human, but stores data as a machine.
